Acquisition Terms & Provenance

The acquisition of Bradley Caldon furniture represents an investment in artistic excellence and cultural heritage, governed by principles that ensure the integrity and preservation of each work for future generations.

Acquisition Process

A considered approach to collecting exceptional furniture

I

Initial Inquiry

Serious collectors submit expressions of interest with background information and collecting philosophy.

II

Private Consultation

Qualified inquiries result in confidential discussions regarding available works and collection alignment.

III

Formal Agreement

Acquisition terms are established, including provenance documentation and preservation requirements.

IV

Delivery & Registration

White-glove delivery with formal registration in the Caldon Provenance Registry.

Intellectual Property & Artistic Integrity

Protecting the artistic vision and cultural significance

1

Artistic Authorship

Each piece remains the intellectual property of Bradley Caldon as the creating artist. The acquisition grants ownership of the physical object while preserving all artistic rights.

This includes reproduction rights, attribution requirements, and artistic integrity protections similar to those governing fine art acquisitions.

2

Modification Prohibition

No structural modifications, alterations, or customizations may be made to any piece without explicit written consent from the atelier.

This preserves the artistic integrity and ensures the work remains an authentic representation of the artist's vision and craftsmanship.

3

Attribution Requirements

All public displays, publications, or references must include proper attribution to Bradley Caldon and the year of creation.

This ensures the cultural and historical record remains accurate and protects the artistic legacy of each work.

Resale Rights & Market Participation

Maintaining market integrity and artistic value

1

Right of First Refusal

The atelier reserves the right of first refusal on any proposed resale, ensuring works may return to the collection when appropriate.

This right must be exercised within 30 days of written notification and at the proposed sale price to third parties.

2

Approved Venues

Resales must occur through established auction houses, recognized dealers, or direct private sale with atelier notification.

This ensures proper documentation, authentication, and maintenance of provenance records for future collectors.

3

Droit de Suite

A resale royalty of 3% is due to the artist on secondary market transactions exceeding the original acquisition price.

This artist's resale right recognizes the ongoing cultural contribution and supports continued artistic production.

Conservation & Preservation

Ensuring longevity for future generations

1

Environmental Standards

Pieces must be maintained in stable environments with appropriate temperature, humidity, and light controls as specified in the conservation guidelines.

Detailed care instructions are provided with each acquisition, covering daily maintenance and long-term preservation requirements.

2

Professional Conservation

Any restoration or conservation work must be performed by qualified furniture conservators with prior consultation with the atelier.

This ensures appropriate techniques are used and maintains the integrity of original materials and construction methods.

3

Inspection Rights

The atelier reserves the right to inspect pieces periodically to ensure proper care and maintenance standards are being met.

Inspections are conducted with reasonable notice and aim to support collectors in optimal care practices.
