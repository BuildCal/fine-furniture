Acquisition Terms & Provenance

The acquisition of Bradley Caldon furniture represents an investment in functional artistic excellence— works designed to be lived with, used, and appreciated daily while maintaining their integrity and cultural significance for future generations.

Acquisition Process

A considered approach to collecting exceptional furniture

I

Initial Inquiry

Serious collectors submit expressions of interest with background information and collecting philosophy.

II

Private Consultation

Qualified inquiries result in confidential discussions regarding available works and collection alignment.

III

Formal Agreement

Acquisition terms are established, including provenance documentation and preservation requirements.

IV

Delivery & Registration

White-glove delivery with formal registration in the Caldon Provenance Registry.

Intellectual Property & Artistic Integrity

Protecting the artistic vision and cultural significance

1

Artistic Authorship

Each piece remains the intellectual property of Bradley Caldon as the creating artist. The acquisition grants ownership of the physical object while preserving all artistic rights.

This includes reproduction rights, attribution requirements, and artistic integrity protections similar to those governing fine art acquisitions.

2

Modification Prohibition

No structural modifications, alterations, or customizations may be made to any piece without explicit written consent from the atelier.

This preserves the artistic integrity and ensures the work remains an authentic representation of the artist's vision and craftsmanship.

3

Attribution Requirements

All public displays, publications, or references must include proper attribution to Bradley Caldon and the year of creation.

This ensures the cultural and historical record remains accurate and protects the artistic legacy of each work.

Exhibition Rights & Cultural Participation

Enhancing cultural significance through public presentation

1

Artist's Exhibition Rights

The atelier reserves the right to request loans for significant exhibitions, retrospectives, or cultural presentations that enhance the artistic legacy and public appreciation of the work.

Exhibition requests are made with minimum 6 months notice, and all transportation, insurance, and conservation costs are covered by the exhibiting institution.

2

Collector Benefits

Participation in exhibitions enhances provenance, increases cultural significance, and provides professional conservation assessment at no cost to the collector.

Collectors receive detailed condition reports, professional photography for their records, and recognition in exhibition materials and catalogues.

3

Reasonable Participation

Exhibition loans are limited to one request every five years per piece, with consideration for the collector's personal use and the significance of the proposed exhibition.

Collectors may decline loans that conflict with personal use or for works requiring conservation. All loans include comprehensive insurance and white-glove handling.

Resale Rights & Market Participation

Maintaining market integrity and artistic value

1

Right of First Refusal

The atelier reserves the right of first refusal on any proposed resale, ensuring works may return to the collection when appropriate.

This right must be exercised within 30 days of written notification and at the proposed sale price to third parties.

2

Approved Venues

Resales must occur through established auction houses, recognized dealers, or direct private sale with atelier notification.

This ensures proper documentation, authentication, and maintenance of provenance records for future collectors.

3

Droit de Suite

A resale royalty of 3% is due to the artist on secondary market transactions exceeding the original acquisition price.

This artist's resale right recognizes the ongoing cultural contribution and supports continued artistic production.

Care & Functional Use

Designed for daily appreciation and mindful use

1

Intended Use Philosophy

Each piece is crafted for daily functional use as living art. The furniture is designed to develop character and patina through thoughtful use while maintaining structural integrity.

Regular use enhances the natural beauty of the materials and honors the craftsman's intention that these works serve both aesthetic and practical purposes.

2

Mindful Care Standards

While designed for use, pieces require thoughtful care including protection from extreme conditions, regular maintenance of finishes, and appropriate placement away from direct heat sources.

Detailed care instructions cover daily use guidelines, periodic maintenance schedules, and seasonal care recommendations to ensure longevity.

3

Professional Maintenance

Periodic professional assessment and maintenance ensure optimal condition. The atelier provides guidance on qualified furniture conservators familiar with these works.

Annual or bi-annual professional care maintains finish quality and addresses normal wear patterns that enhance rather than diminish the piece's character.
